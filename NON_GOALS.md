# NON_GOALS

## Absolute Exclusions

This document defines what the identity-root system explicitly does NOT provide, support, or intend to address.

### Out of Scope

- **Continuous Integration**: No CI pipelines, automated testing, or build automation will be maintained
- **Secret Management**: No key storage, secret injection, or credential management systems
- **README Documentation**: No user-facing documentation, tutorials, or getting-started guides beyond governance
- **Future Phases**: No roadmap, feature backlog, or planned enhancements beyond Base120 v1.0
- **Automated Governance**: No policy engines, automated compliance checking, or runtime enforcement
- **Anonymous Identity**: No support for zero-knowledge proofs, anonymity sets, or unlinkable credentials
- **Federated Trust**: No cross-domain trust bridges, certificate authorities, or PKI integration
- **Identity Recovery**: No social recovery, backup mechanisms, or account reset procedures
- **Ambient Authority**: No capability-based security models that don't require explicit delegation
- **Mutable History**: No log compaction, history rewriting, or retroactive authority changes
- **Implicit Permissions**: No role-based access control without explicit cryptographic grants
- **Backward Compatibility**: No support for versions prior to Base120 v1.0
- **Forward Migration**: No version upgrade paths or protocol evolution mechanisms
- **Partial Delegation**: No support for subset or conditional authority grants
- **Time Travel**: No ability to query or validate historical states at arbitrary points
- **Soft Delete**: No deactivation or suspension of identities without full revocation
- **Grace Periods**: No delayed enforcement or probationary states for policy violations
- **Default Permissions**: No implicit grants or inherited capabilities without explicit edges
- **Trusted Intermediaries**: No reliance on third-party validators or attestation services
- **Performance Optimization**: No caching layers, indexing, or query optimization beyond baseline requirements
